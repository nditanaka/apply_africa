Exciting news! The 2017 application for the Yale Young African Scholars Program is now available! Yale Young African Scholars Program (YYAS) is a high-intensity academic and leadership program designed for African secondary school students who have the talent, drive, energy, and ideas to make meaningful impacts as young leaders, even before they begin their university studies. This free program brings together students from across Africa in this seven-day, residential program and prepares students for the demanding U.S. university and financial aid application process and requirements. Students accepted into this program should expect to study in a rigorous tertiary education environment. YYAS is an official program of Yale University, in proud partnership with the Higherlife Foundation.

The application deadline is March 16, 2017, at 11:59pm EST.

Prospective applicants must be citizens of an African country and currently attend school on the African continent. Applicants will have two years left before completing high school and be between the ages of 14-18. Thanks to the generous support of the HigherLife Foundation, YYAS is free of cost to all accepted students. We are also able to offer travel stipends for students from low-income backgrounds.

Rotary Youth Exchange Program

Deadline: December 30, 2016

1468540800

Organizer: Rotary International
Website: Apply here
Eligibility: 16-18 years

Rotary Youth Exchange Students chosen will spend the year 2017 in another country, attending secondary school and living with host families under the auspices of the Rotary organization. The major objectives of the programme are to further international goodwill and understanding, to enable students to advance their wider education by studying and living in a foreign environment, to broaden their outlook, to act as ‚Äòambassadors‚Äô for Zimbabwe, and to study foreign lifestyles and culture. Students generally stay with 3 host families during their year abroad. January 2017 is the month of departure. Age Limits Students wishing to apply should generally be over 16 and under 18 years of age on 1st January 2017. Residency Applicants must be citizens or permanent residents of Zimbabwe.

Costs

Parents of exchange students are required to pay the cost of travel to and from the host country, health and liability insurance, and other minor costs incurred for preparation and orientation for the year away. Costs are averaged for all destinations. The host Rotary club in the destination country will provide full board with host families, schooling and basic pocket money for the year. The overall cost of sending students overseas is very difficult to predict in advance of booking because of inflation and exchange rates. Our most recent estimate, which will be updated by the time of selection, is +/-US$3,500.00. This excludes money for activities, trips and tours during the year.Activities and tours are voluntary, but are usually very beneficial to the students.
